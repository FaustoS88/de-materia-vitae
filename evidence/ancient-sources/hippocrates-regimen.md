# Hippocrates — On the Regimen (~400 BC)

> Translated from the Loeb Classical Library. *Hippocrates*, Vol. IV. Harvard University Press.

---

## On Walking

**Aphorisms, Section IV, Aphorism 3**

> "Walking is man's best medicine."

> "All parts of the body which have a function, if used in moderation and exercised in labors to which each is accustomed, become healthy and well-developed. But if they are left unused, they become liable to disease, stunted in growth, and rapidly age."

**Relevance:** The single most quoted line in exercise medicine. Modern meta-analyses of 15+ cohort studies confirm walking is the most accessible, evidence-backed intervention for all-cause mortality reduction.

---

## On Exercise

**On Regimen, Book I**

> "Exercise before meals is beneficial for warming the body, promoting digestion, and strengthening the constitution. All parts of the body should be exercised, but not to exhaustion."

> "A person who is sedentary, even if well-fed, is less healthy than one who exercises moderately and eats simply."

**Relevance:** Pre-meal exercise is now known to improve postprandial glucose metabolism by 20–30%. The warning against sedentary life, even with good nutrition, mirrors modern findings that sitting time independently predicts mortality regardless of exercise.

---

## On Sleep

**On Regimen, Book I**

> "Sleep and wakefulness in excess are both unwholesome. Sleep should be taken in the night, and one should rise with the sun."

> "The bed should be soft, the covering light. One should sleep on the right side first, then turn to the left."

**Relevance:** Circadian-aligned sleep (nighttime, consistent schedule) is now known to optimize melatonin production, glymphatic clearance, and hormonal rhythms. The right-side sleeping recommendation is echoed in modern gastroenterology (reduces acid reflux).

---

## On Fasting

**Aphorisms, Section II, Aphorism 10**

> "To eat when sick is to feed the illness. Fasting is the greatest remedy."

**On Regimen, Book I**

> "It is better to go without food for a day than to eat when the previous meal is not yet digested."

**Relevance:** Fasting activates autophagy (cellular cleanup), improves insulin sensitivity, and gives the gut and immune system a rest. The principle of not eating until the previous meal is digested aligns with modern time-restricted eating protocols.

---

## On Diet and Medicine

**Aphorisms, Section I**

> "Let food be thy medicine and medicine be thy food."

**On the Regimen, Book II**

> "Water should be clear, light, odorless, and free from sediment. Boiling and filtering through cloth improves quality."

> "Soured milk and fermented drinks are more easily digested and strengthen the body."

**Relevance:** The foundational principle of nutritional medicine. Water purification through boiling/filtering was a practical public health measure. Fermented dairy as digestive aid aligns with modern probiotic science.

---

## On Emotions and Health

**On Regimen, Book I**

> "Tranquility and rest are the chief things. The soul should be trained to remain calm and undisturbed."

> "The effects of grief, anger, and fear on the body are greater than most people realize."

**Relevance:** Chronic emotional stress elevates cortisol, drives inflammation, shortens telomeres, and accelerates biological aging. Modern psychoneuroimmunology has validated what Hippocrates observed clinically.

---

## On Light and Darkness

**Airs, Waters, Places, Chapter 2**

> "The light of the sun is the source of health, and its absence is the source of disease. The daily rhythm of light and darkness governs the body's rhythms."

**Relevance:** Circadian biology. Light exposure sets the suprachiasmatic nucleus, which governs cortisol, melatonin, metabolism, immune function, and DNA repair cycles. Disruption is classified by the WHO as a probable carcinogen.

## Summary: Hippocratic Principles

| Principle | Ancient Statement | Modern Equivalent |
|-----------|------------------|------------------|
| **Movement** | "Walking is man's best medicine" | 7,000–10,000 steps/day reduces mortality 50%+ |
| **Exercise** | "Use it or lose it" | Sarcopenia begins at 30; resistance training reverses |
| **Sleep** | "Night sleep, rise with sun" | Circadian alignment optimizes all physiological rhythms |
| **Fasting** | "Fasting is the greatest remedy" | Autophagy, AMPK activation, mTOR inhibition |
| **Food as medicine** | "Let food be thy medicine" | Nutritional epidemiology confirms diet as primary health determinant |
| **Water quality** | "Clear, light, pure water" | Water contaminants cause neurotoxicity, cancer, developmental damage |
| **Emotional health** | "Tranquility is chief" | Chronic stress → inflammation → accelerated aging |
| **Light rhythm** | "Light governs the body's rhythms" | Circadian disruption → metabolic, immune, cancer risk |
