# Avicenna — The Canon of Medicine (1025 AD)

> Translated from Gruner, O.C. (1930). *Avicenna: A Treatise on the Canon of Medicine*. Luzac & Co.

---

## On Exercise

**Book I, Chapter 4**

> "Exercise is that which is deliberate and purposeful, and it is necessary for the preservation of health. It should be practiced daily."

> "Exercise warms the body, strengthens the muscles, improves digestion, and lifts the spirit. It should be moderate and regular, not excessive and sporadic."

**Relevance:** Avicenna emphasized daily, moderate exercise — consistent with modern findings that consistent moderate activity (walking) outperforms sporadic intense exercise for longevity.

---

## On Sleep

**Book I, Chapter 5**

> "Sleep is necessary for digestion, growth, and the preservation of strength. It should be taken on the right side, in darkness and silence."

> "Strong emotions, if prolonged, alter the temperament of the body and cause disease. The physician must attend to the patient's emotional state."

**Relevance:** Sleep in darkness aligns with modern understanding of melatonin production. The connection between prolonged emotions and physical disease is now the field of psychoneuroimmunology.

---

## On Digestion and Meal Timing

**Book I, Chapter 7**

> "Digestion must be allowed to complete before the next meal. Three days are needed for full digestion of heavy foods."

> "One should not eat until truly hungry, and the stomach should be given rest between meals. The greatest harm comes from eating without need."

**Relevance:** While "three days for full digestion" is an overestimate (transit time is 24–72 hours), the principle of not eating until the previous meal is digested aligns with modern intermittent fasting and time-restricted eating.

---

## On Nuts

**Book II, "On Foods"**

> "Nuts are strengthening for the brain and nerves. Walnuts specifically are good for head ailments. Almonds nourish the body but require strong digestion."

**Relevance:** Walnuts and brain health — consistent with modern findings that walnuts are the nut most associated with cognitive protection (highest omega-3 ALA, polyphenols, and melatonin among nuts).

---

## On Fermented Foods

**Book III**

> "Fermented milk products improve digestion, strengthen the stomach, and are beneficial for the constitution."

**Relevance:** Fermented dairy as a digestive aid — consistent with modern probiotic science showing fermented foods increase microbiome diversity and reduce inflammatory markers.

---

## On Sun Protection

**Book III, "On the Eye" and Book I**

> "Excessive sun exposure damages the skin, causes premature aging, and should be moderated. Protection with oils and coverings is advisable."

> "The eyes should be protected from strong light and from reading in dim light. Rest the eyes frequently."

**Relevance:** Recognition of UV damage to skin centuries before the discovery of UV radiation. Eye protection from intense light is consistent with modern understanding of blue light damage to the retina.

---

## On Garlic and Parasites

**Book II, "On Simple Drugs"**

> "Garlic is effective against worms when taken internally. It warms the body, strengthens digestion, and is beneficial for the constitution."

> "Lupin and pomegranate root also expel intestinal parasites."

**Relevance:** Garlic as an anthelmintic — confirmed by clinical trials showing garlic extract cleared Giardia in children within 3 days.

## On the Six Essentials of Health

**Book I, Introduction**

Avicenna identified six factors essential for health, which he called the "six non-naturals" (things not innate to the body but necessary for its maintenance):

| # | Essential | Avicenna's Description | Modern Equivalent |
|---|-----------|----------------------|------------------|
| 1 | **Air** | Quality of the air breathed | Air quality, oxygenation, pollution exposure |
| 2 | **Food and drink** | Quantity, quality, and timing | Nutrition, caloric intake, meal timing |
| 3 | **Movement and rest** | Exercise and sleep | Physical activity, sleep quality, sedentary time |
| 4 | **Retention and evacuation** | Bowel regularity, elimination | Gut health, detoxification pathways |
| 5 | **Sleep and wakefulness** | Duration and timing | Circadian rhythm, sleep architecture |
| 6 | **Emotions** | Mental states and passions | Stress, anxiety, depression, emotional regulation |

**Relevance:** This framework is remarkably similar to the modern biopsychosocial model of health. All six factors are now evidence-based determinants of healthspan and lifespan.
