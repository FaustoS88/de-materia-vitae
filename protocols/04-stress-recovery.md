# 04 — Stress & Recovery

> **Category:** Stress & Recovery  
> **Evidence Grade:** ★★★★☆  
> **Last Updated:** 2026-04-06

---

Chronic stress is not a feeling — it's a physiological state that accelerates aging at the cellular level. Managing it is not optional for longevity.

## I. Meditation

**Dose:** 10–20 minutes daily

### Modern Evidence

| Study | Finding | Reference |
|-------|---------|-----------|
| Goyal et al., 2014, meta-analysis (47 RCTs), *JAMA Intern Med* | Meditation programs improve anxiety, depression, and pain with effect sizes comparable to antidepressants (0.3–0.4) | PMID: 24395196 |
| Epel et al., 2009, *Mol Psychiatry* | Chronic stress associated with shorter telomeres; meditation increases telomerase activity | PMID: 29494257 |
| Kaliman et al., 2014, *Psychoneuroendocrinology* | Mindfulness practice downregulates NF-κB pathway, reduces pro-inflammatory gene expression, increases histone deacetylase activity | PMID: 24485481 |
| Chaix et al., 2020, *Transl Psychiatry* | Meditation practice associated with slower epigenetic aging (Horvath clock) in experienced meditators | PMID: 28889075 |

**Evidence Summary:** ★★★★☆ (Very Good) — Strong evidence for mental health outcomes; growing evidence for biological aging markers.

### Mechanism

- **HPA axis regulation:** Reduces chronic cortisol output; normalizes diurnal cortisol rhythm
- **Telomerase activation:** Increases enzyme that maintains telomere length (cellular aging clock)
- **Inflammation downregulation:** Reduces NF-κB activity, IL-6, CRP, TNF-α
- **Default mode network:** Reduces mind-wandering (DMN hyperactivity correlated with unhappiness)
- **Vagal tone:** Increases heart rate variability (HRV), a biomarker of resilience and longevity

### Protocol

| Parameter | Recommendation |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Type** | Mindfulness (attention to breath, notice mind-wandering, return) |
| **Duration** | 10–20 minutes daily |
| **Timing** | Morning (sets tone for day) or evening (unwinds nervous system) |
| **Consistency** | 10 min daily > 60 min once per week |
| **Apps** | Waking Up, Headspace, Insight Timer (free), or simply a timer |

**How to start (simplest version):**
1. Sit comfortably, set timer for 10 minutes
2. Focus attention on the sensation of breathing (nostrils, chest, or abdomen)
3. When you notice your mind has wandered (it will), gently return attention to breath
4. That's it. The "noticing and returning" IS the practice — it's not a failure

## II. Breathwork

**Dose:** 5–10 minutes, 1–2× daily

### Modern Evidence

| Study | Finding | Reference |
|-------|---------|-----------|
| Zaccaro et al., 2018, *Front Hum Neurosci* (systematic review) | Slow breathing increases HRV, reduces anxiety and depression, improves autonomic balance | PMID: 30245619 |
| Steffen et al., 2017, *Front Public Health* | Resonance-frequency breathing (6 breaths/min) maximizes HRV and baroreflex sensitivity | PMID: 28890890 |
| Lehrer & Gevirtz, 2014, *Front Psychol* | HRV biofeedback training (via slow breathing) effective for asthma, depression, anxiety, fibromyalgia | PMID: 25101026 |

**Evidence Summary:** ★★★★☆ (Very Good) — Strong autonomic nervous system effects; growing clinical applications.

### Protocol

#### Box Breathing (for acute stress)
- Inhale 4 seconds → Hold 4 seconds → Exhale 4 seconds → Hold 4 seconds
- 5–10 cycles
- Activates parasympathetic nervous system within 2–3 minutes

#### 4-7-8 Breathing (for sleep onset)
- Inhale through nose 4 seconds → Hold 7 seconds → Exhale through mouth 8 seconds
- 4–8 cycles
- Extends exhalation to maximize vagal activation

#### Resonance Breathing (for daily practice)
- 6 breaths per minute (5 seconds in, 5 seconds out)
- 10–20 minutes
- Maximizes HRV and baroreflex gain
