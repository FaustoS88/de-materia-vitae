## I. Routine Deworming

**Dose:** Natural protocol quarterly OR pharmaceutical protocol biannually

### Ancient Source

| Source | Text | Reference |
|--------|------|-----------|
| Dioscorides, *De Materia Medica*, ~60 AD | "Wormwood (Artemisia) expels intestinal worms. Garlic and pumpkin seeds are effective against parasites." | Book III, Chapter 116; Book II, Chapter 130 |
| Avicenna, *Canon of Medicine*, 1025 AD | "Garlic is effective against worms when taken internally. Lupin and pomegranate root also expel intestinal parasites." | Book II, "On Simple Drugs" |
| *Sushruta Samhita*, ~600 BC | "Garlic, pomegranate bark, and wormwood are effective against intestinal worms (krimi)." | Sutrasthana Chapter 45 |
| Ebers Papyrus, ~1550 BC (Ancient Egypt) | "For worms: garlic, figs, and dates administered as a draught." | Paragraph 251 |

### Modern Evidence

### Mechanism (Why It Matters for Longevity)

Even subclinical parasitic presence can contribute to:
- **Chronic low-grade inflammation** → elevated IL-6, TNF-α, CRP (linked to accelerated aging)
- **Nutrient malabsorption** → B12, iron, zinc, magnesium competition (critical for DNA repair, mitochondrial function)
- **Gut dysbiosis** → reduced SCFA production, impaired gut barrier integrity
- **Immune system burden** → Th2-skewed response diverts resources from cancer/virus surveillance

### Protocol

**See the full protocol for complete details:** [Deworming Protocol](../deworming-protocol.md)

Quick version:
- **Natural:** 14-day protocol using garlic, pumpkin seeds, papaya seeds + herbal combo (wormwood, black walnut, cloves) — quarterly
- **Pharmaceutical:** Albendazole 400 mg single dose, repeated in 14 days — biannually
- **Hybrid:** Natural quarterly + pharmaceutical biannually (staggered)

## II. Testing Schedule

### Recommended Annual Biomarkers

| Test | What it tells you | Target | Frequency |
|------|------------------|--------|-----------|
| **CBC with differential** | Anemia, infection, eosinophil elevation (parasite marker) | Eosinophils <5% | Annual |
| **Comprehensive metabolic panel** | Liver, kidney, glucose, electrolytes | All within range | Annual |
| **Lipid panel** | Cardiovascular risk | ApoB <80 mg/dL, HDL >50 | Annual |
| **HbA1c** | Average blood glucose (3 months) | <5.4% | Annual |
| **hs-CRP** | Systemic inflammation | <1.0 mg/L | Annual |
| **Vitamin D (25-OH)** | Immune, bone, mood regulation | 40–60 ng/mL | Annual |
| **Ferritin** | Iron stores (deficiency → fatigue; excess → oxidative stress) | 50–150 ng/mL | Annual |
| **B12 + Folate** | Methylation, neurological function | B12 >500 pg/mL | Annual |
| **TSH + Free T3/T4** | Thyroid function (fatigue, metabolism, mood) | TSH 0.5–2.5 mIU/L | Annual |
| **Omega-3 index** | Cardiovascular mortality predictor | >8% | Every 2 years |

### Advanced / Every 3–5 Years

| Test | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| **ApoB** | More accurate cardiovascular risk than LDL alone |
| **Lp(a)** | Genetic cardiovascular risk (one-time test) |
| **GI-MAP (stool PCR)** | Comprehensive parasite, bacteria, fungi detection |
| **Dexa scan** | Bone density, body composition |
| **Coronary calcium scan** | Atherosclerosis detection (age 40+) |
| **Biological age clock** | GrimAge or DunedinPACE (epigenetic aging rate) |

## III. Time-Restricted Eating

**Dose:** 8–10 hour eating window daily

### Ancient Source

| Source | Text | Reference |
|--------|------|-----------|
| Hippocrates, *Aphorisms*, ~400 BC | "To eat when sick is to feed the illness. Fasting is the greatest remedy." | Aphorism I, 10 |
| Avicenna, *Canon of Medicine*, 1025 AD | "Digestion must be allowed to complete before the next meal. Three days are needed for full digestion of heavy foods." | Book I, Chapter 7 |
| *Charaka Samhita*, ~400 BC | "One should eat only when the previous meal is digested. Improper timing of meals is the root of all diseases." | Sutrasthana Chapter 5 |

### Modern Evidence

| Study | Finding | Reference |
|-------|---------|-----------|
| Wilkinson et al., 2020, *Cell Metab* | 10-hour TRE in humans improves metabolic health, reduces weight, improves blood pressure | PMID: 31813824 |
| Sutton et al., 2018, *Cell Metab* | Early TRE (6-hr window, early dinner) improves insulin sensitivity, blood pressure, oxidative stress | PMID: 29754952 |
| de Cabo & Mattson, 2019, *NEJM* (review) | Intermittent fasting improves glucose regulation, stress resistance, reduces inflammation | PMID: 31881139 |

**Evidence Summary:** ★★★★☆ (Very Good) — Strong metabolic benefits; longevity extension proven in animals, emerging in humans.

### Mechanism

- **Autophagy:** Cellular cleanup process increases after ~16 hours of fasting (Nobel Prize 2016, Yoshinori Ohsumi)
- **AMPK activation:** Energy sensor that promotes mitochondrial biogenesis, fat oxidation, glucose uptake
- **mTOR inhibition:** Growth pathway suppression linked to longevity in every species studied
- **Circadian alignment:** Eating window aligned with daylight hours improves metabolic hormone rhythms

### Protocol

| Parameter | Recommendation |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Window** | 8–10 hours (e.g., 10 AM – 6 PM, or 9 AM – 5 PM) |
| **Fasting period** | 14–16 hours |
| **During fasting** | Water, black coffee, plain tea only |
| **Consistency** | Same window daily (circadian alignment) |
| **Who should avoid** | Underweight, pregnant/nursing, eating disorder history, type 1 diabetes (without medical supervision) |
