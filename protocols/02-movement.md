# 02 — Movement

> **Category:** Movement  
> **Evidence Grade:** ★★★★★  
> **Last Updated:** 2026-04-06

Movement as medicine: the two practices with the strongest evidence for healthspan.

## I. Resistance Training

**Dose:** 2–3 sessions per week, 30–45 minutes

### Modern Evidence

| Study | Finding | Reference |
|-------|---------|-----------|
| Leong et al., 2015, *Lancet* (PURE study, 140K adults) | Grip strength is a stronger predictor of all-cause mortality than systolic blood pressure | PMID: 26432304 |
| Srikanthan & Karlamangla, 2014, *Am J Med* | Muscle mass index inversely associated with all-cause mortality in elderly | PMID: 24508377 |
| Peterson et al., 2011, *Prev Med* | Resistance training associated with 23% reduction in all-cause mortality | PMID: 21195105 |

**Evidence Summary:** ★★★★★ (Excellent) — Grip strength is one of the most validated biomarkers of aging.

### Mechanism

- **Muscle as endocrine organ:** Myokines (IL-6, irisin, BDNF) released during contraction have systemic anti-inflammatory and neuroprotective effects
- **Glucose disposal:** Muscle is the primary site of insulin-mediated glucose uptake; more muscle = better metabolic health
- **Bone density:** Mechanical loading stimulates osteoblast activity; prevents osteoporosis
- **Mitochondrial density:** Resistance training increases mitochondrial content and function in muscle cells
- **Fall prevention:** Strength + balance = independence in later life

### Protocol

| Parameter | Recommendation |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Frequency** | 2–3 sessions/week |
| **Exercises** | Compound movements: squats, deadlifts, rows, overhead press, pull-ups/push-ups |
| **Sets/Reps** | 3 sets of 5–12 reps per exercise |
| **Progression** | Progressive overload — increase weight or reps each week |
| **Rest** | 2–3 min between sets |
| **Minimum effective dose** | 2 sessions/week, full body, 3–5 compound exercises, 3 sets each |

### Equipment Needed

| Tier | Equipment | Cost (US) |
|------|-----------|-----------|
| **Foundation** | Bodyweight only (push-ups, squats, lunges, planks) | $0 |
| **Essential** | Resistance bands + pull-up bar | ~$25-40 |
| **Complete** | Dumbbells or kettlebell set + adjustable bench | ~$200-500 |

### Quick Reference

| | |
|---|---|
| **What** | Compound resistance training |
| **How much** | 2–3×/week, 30–45 min |
| **Evidence** | ★★★★★ |
| **Ancient source** | Hippocrates, Avicenna |
| **Key biomarker** | Grip strength |
| **Watch out for** | Start light, focus on form; injury derails everything |

## II. Walking

**Dose:** 7,000–10,000 steps daily

### Modern Evidence

| Study | Finding | Reference |
|-------|---------|-----------|
| Paluch et al., 2021, *JAMA Netw Open* | Each 1,000-step increment associated with 6–36% lower mortality; benefits plateau ~7,000–10,000 steps | PMID: 34287614 |
| Lee et al., 2019, *JAMA Intern Med* | 4,400 steps/day associated with 41% lower mortality vs. 2,700; benefit continues to ~7,500 | PMID: 31135876 |
| Saint-Maurice et al., 2020, *JAMA* | 8,000 steps/day associated with 51% lower all-cause mortality vs. 4,000; 75% lower cardiovascular mortality | PMID: 32282010 |

**Evidence Summary:** ★★★★★ (Excellent) — Walking is the most studied and most accessible health intervention.

### Mechanism

- **Cardiovascular fitness:** Improves VO2 max, a strong predictor of longevity
- **Insulin sensitivity:** Post-meal walking significantly blunts glucose spikes
- **Bone density:** Weight-bearing activity stimulates bone remodeling
- **Lymphatic drainage:** Walking is the primary driver of lymph flow (no pump, relies on movement)
- **Mood & cognition:** Increases BDNF, serotonin, endorphins; reduces cortisol
- **Visceral fat reduction:** Moderate-intensity walking preferentially reduces visceral adipose tissue

### Protocol

| Parameter | Recommendation |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Target** | 7,000–10,000 steps/day |
| **Minimum** | 4,000 steps (mortality benefit starts here) |
| **Post-meal** | 10 min walk after each meal (blunts glucose spikes by 20–30%) |
| **Pace** | Brisk (able to talk, not sing) — ~100 steps/min |
| **Terrain** | Varied (hills, uneven ground) engages more stabilizing muscles |
| **Footwear** | Invest in quality, comfortable shoes — the single most important piece of equipment |

### Integration Strategies

- Walking meetings
- Park further, take stairs
- Walk after every meal (even 5 min helps)
- Morning walk to set circadian rhythm (outdoor light exposure)
- Evening walk for digestion and sleep preparation

### Quick Reference

| | |
|---|---|
| **What** | Daily walking |
| **How much** | 7,000–10,000 steps/day |
| **Evidence** | ★★★★★ |
| **Ancient source** | Hippocrates: "Walking is man's best medicine" |
| **Cost** | Free (shoes are the only investment) |
| **Key insight** | Consistency > intensity. Daily walking > occasional running. |
