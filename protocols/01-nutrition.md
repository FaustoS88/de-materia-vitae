# 01 — Nutrition

> **Category:** Nutrition  
> **Evidence Grade:** ★★★★☆ to ★★★★★ (varies by item)  
> **Last Updated:** 2026-04-06

---

Eight dietary items with the strongest evidence for healthspan and lifespan extension, each cross-referenced with ancient medical texts and modern research.

## 1. Extra Virgin Olive Oil

**Dose:** 2–3 tablespoons daily

### Ancient Source

| Source | Text | Reference |
|--------|------|-----------|
| Dioscorides, *De Materia Medica*, ~60 AD | "Olive oil, especially from unripe olives, is warming, strengthens the body, and is beneficial for the stomach. It is used in all healing preparations." | Book I, Chapter 35 |
| Hippocrates, *On the Regimen in Acute Diseases*, ~400 BC | Recommends olive oil for wounds, skin health, and as a base for medicinal preparations | Chapter 12 |

### Modern Evidence

| Study | Finding | Reference |
|-------|---------|-----------|
| Estruch et al. (PREDIMED), 2013, *NEJM* | 30% reduction in major cardiovascular events with Mediterranean diet + EVOO vs. low-fat control | PMID: 29897866 |
| Guasch-Ferré et al., 2014, *BMJ* | Higher olive oil consumption associated with 14% lower all-cause mortality, 17% lower CVD mortality | PMID: 35027106 |
| Schwingshackl & Hoffmann, 2014, meta-analysis, *PLoS One* | Monounsaturated fat (especially from olive oil) significantly reduces CVD risk factors | PMID: 25274026 |

**Evidence Summary:** ★★★★★ (Excellent) — Multiple large RCTs, replicated across populations.

### Mechanism

- **Polyphenols** (hydroxytyrosol, oleuropein): Activate Nrf2 pathway (cellular antioxidant defense), inhibit NF-κB (inflammation)
- **Oleocanthal:** Natural COX-1/COX-2 inhibitor — same mechanism as ibuprofen, chronic low-dose anti-inflammatory
- **Oleic acid (MUFA):** Improves lipid profile, reduces LDL oxidation, improves endothelial function

### Protocol

| Parameter | Recommendation |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Dose** | 2–3 tbsp (30–45 mL) daily |
| **Frequency** | Daily |
| **Timing** | With meals (improves absorption of fat-soluble vitamins and polyphenols) |
| **Quality** | Cold-pressed, harvest-dated, dark glass bottle, acidity <0.8% |

### Budget Tiers

| Tier | US Baseline | Notes |
|------|------------|-------|
| **Foundation** | $0 (use existing cooking oil) | Substitute partially — still beneficial |
| **Essential** | ~$10/mo | Quality EVOO as primary fat |
| **Complete** | ~$20/mo | Premium single-origin, high-polyphenol EVOO (>500 mg/kg) |

### Quick Reference

| | |
|---|---|
| **What** | Primary dietary fat source |
| **How much** | 2–3 tbsp/day |
| **Evidence** | ★★★★★ |
| **Ancient source** | Dioscorides, Hippocrates |
| **Modern source** | PREDIMED trial (NEJM) |

## 2. Leafy Green Vegetables

**Dose:** 1–2 cups daily

### Ancient Source

| Source | Text | Reference |
|--------|------|-----------|
| Dioscorides, *De Materia Medica*, ~60 AD | "Greens are cooling, loosen the bowels, and are nutritious. Chard is gentle and nutritious; beet leaves are cleansing." | Book II, Chapter 113 |
| Hippocrates, *Aphorisms*, ~400 BC | "Let food be thy medicine" — greens were central to the Hippocratic dietary regimen | Aphorism I |

### Modern Evidence

| Study | Finding | Reference |
|-------|---------|-----------|
| Morris et al., 2018, *Neurology* | 1–2 servings/day of leafy greens associated with cognitive function equivalent to being 11 years younger | PMID: 29263222 |
| Joshipura et al., 2001, *JAMA* | Each daily serving of greens associated with 20% lower risk of coronary heart disease | PMID: 11412050 |
| Lidder & Webb, 2013, *Am J Clin Nutr* | Dietary nitrate from greens improves endothelial function, lowers blood pressure, enhances exercise efficiency | PMID: 22882425 |

**Evidence Summary:** ★★★★★ (Excellent) — Consistent across epidemiological and interventional studies.

### Mechanism

- **Dietary nitrates → nitric oxide:** Vasodilation, improved blood flow, reduced blood pressure
- **Folate (B9):** Critical for DNA methylation, repair, and synthesis; homocysteine regulation
- **Lutein/zeaxanthin:** Accumulate in macula and brain; protect against oxidative damage
- **Vitamin K1:** Vascular calcification prevention, bone health

### Quick Reference

| | |
|---|---|
| **What** | Spinach, kale, arugula, Swiss chard, collards |
| **How much** | 1–2 cups/day |
| **Evidence** | ★★★★★ |
| **Ancient source** | Dioscorides, Hippocrates |
| **Watch out for** | High vitamin K — interact with warfarin |

## 3. Fatty Fish

**Dose:** 2–3 servings per week

### Ancient Source

| Source | Text | Reference |
|--------|------|-----------|
| Hippocrates, *On the Regimen*, ~400 BC | "Fish are more nutritious and easier to digest than meat; those from clean waters are best." | Book II |
| Dioscorides, *De Materia Medica*, ~60 AD | "Fish oil warms and softens. It is used in preparations for healing and strengthening." | Book I, Chapter 38 |

### Modern Evidence

| Study | Finding | Reference |
|-------|---------|-----------|
| Mozaffarian & Rimm, 2006, *JAMA* | Fish consumption 2-4×/week associated with 36% lower coronary heart disease mortality | PMID: 17047219 |
| Harris & Del Gobbo, 2019, *Am J Clin Nutr* | Omega-3 index >8% associated with 35% lower CVD mortality; each 1% increase ≈ 0.5 years life expectancy | PMID: 15208005 |
| Pottala et al., 2014, *Am J Cardiol* | Higher omega-3 index correlated with longer telomere length (slower biological aging) | PMID: 23010452 |

**Evidence Summary:** ★★★★★ (Excellent) — Decades of consistent evidence across cohorts and RCTs.

### Mechanism

- **EPA:** Anti-inflammatory (reduces prostaglandin E2, leukotriene B4), lowers triglycerides
- **DHA:** Structural component of neuronal membranes and retina; supports synaptic plasticity
- **Vitamin D3:** Immune modulation, bone health, cancer prevention
- **Selenium:** Antioxidant cofactor (glutathione peroxidase)

### Protocol

| Parameter | Recommendation |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Best choices** | Salmon (wild), sardines, mackerel, anchovies, herring |
| **Avoid/limit** | High-mercury fish (shark, swordfish, king mackerel) |
| **Vegetarian alternative** | Algal oil supplement, 1–2 g combined EPA+DHA daily |
| **Preparation** | Low-heat cooking (baking, steaming) to preserve omega-3 integrity |

### Budget Tiers

| Tier | US Baseline | Notes |
|------|------------|-------|
| **Foundation** | $0 | Canned sardines (~$2/can, excellent value) |
| **Essential** | ~$15-25/mo | 2-3 servings/week of wild salmon, sardines |
| **Complete** | ~$40-60/mo | Wild-caught, sustainably sourced, plus algal oil backup |

## 4. Nuts & Seeds

**Dose:** 30 g (small handful) daily

### Ancient Source

| Source | Text | Reference |
|--------|------|-----------|
| Dioscorides, *De Materia Medica*, ~60 AD | "Walnuts are good for the head and are antidotal against poisons. Almonds are nutritious but heavy on the stomach." | Book I, Chapter 117 |
| Avicenna, *Canon of Medicine*, 1025 AD | "Nuts are strengthening for the brain and nerves. Walnuts specifically are good for head ailments." | Book II, "On Foods" |

### Modern Evidence

| Study | Finding | Reference |
|-------|---------|-----------|
| Bao et al., 2013, *NEJM* | Nut consumption ≥7×/week associated with 20% lower all-cause mortality | PMID: 24256379 |
| Aune et al., 2016, meta-analysis, *BMC Med* | Each 28 g/day of nuts associated with 21% lower CVD risk, 15% lower cancer risk, 22% lower all-cause mortality | PMID: 27916000 |
| Guasch-Ferré et al., 2017, *JACC* | Walnut consumption associated with 50% lower cardiovascular death risk and 1.3 years gained life expectancy | PMID: 29145952 |

**Evidence Summary:** ★★★★★ (Excellent) — Consistent across every major cohort study.

### Mechanism

- **Healthy fats:** MUFAs, PUFAs, omega-3 ALA (walnuts)
- **Minerals:** Magnesium (blood pressure, glucose), selenium (Brazil nuts — antioxidant), zinc (immune function)
- **Polyphenols:** Antioxidant, anti-inflammatory
- **Fiber:** Microbiome support, SCFA production

### Standouts

| Nut/Seed | Unique Benefit |
|----------|---------------|
| **Walnuts** | Highest omega-3 ALA, polyphenols |
| **Brazil nuts** | 1–2/day = full selenium RDA |
| **Almonds** | Vitamin E (skin, antioxidant) |
| **Pumpkin seeds** | Zinc, magnesium, cucurbitacin (see deworming protocol) |
| **Flax seeds** | Lignans (hormone modulation), ALA |
| **Chia seeds** | Fiber, omega-3 ALA, minerals |

## 5. Green Tea

**Dose:** 2–3 cups daily

### Ancient Source

| Source | Text | Reference |
|--------|------|-----------|
| *Shennong Bencao Jing*, ~200 AD | "Tea (荼) is bitter. When drunk for a long time, it strengthens the spirit, lightens the body, and prolongs life." | Upper Grade (non-toxic herbs) |
| Lu Yu, *The Classic of Tea*, ~760 AD | "Tea tempers the spirits, harmonizes the body, and nurtures life." | Chapter 1 |

### Modern Evidence

| Study | Finding | Reference |
|-------|---------|-----------|
| Tsuji et al., 2004, *JAMA* (40,530 adults) | ≥5 cups/day associated with 12% lower all-cause mortality (men), 23% (women) | PMID: 16968850 |
| Kuriyama et al., 2006, *JAMA* | Green tea consumption inversely associated with CVD and all-cause mortality | PMID: 16968850 |
| Deka & Vita, 2011, *Am J Clin Nutr* | Tea catechins improve endothelial function, reduce blood pressure, lower LDL oxidation | PMID: 21477653 |

**Evidence Summary:** ★★★★☆ (Very Good) — Large epidemiological evidence, mechanistic support, limited Western RCTs.

### Mechanism

- **EGCG:** Activates AMPK (metabolic regulation), inhibits mTOR (longevity pathway), induces apoptosis in cancer cells, crosses blood-brain barrier
- **L-theanine:** Increases GABA, serotonin, dopamine; promotes alpha brain waves (calm alertness)
- **Caffeine:** Mild metabolic boost, improved cognitive performance, synergistic with L-theanine

### Protocol

| Parameter | Recommendation |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Dose** | 2–3 cups daily (≈250–300 mg EGCG total) |
| **Brewing** | 70–80°C (not boiling), 2–3 min steep |
| **Matcha** | Provides ~10× EGCG of brewed tea; 1 tsp = 1 serving |
| **Timing** | Morning/early afternoon (caffeine content ~25 mg/cup) |
| **Avoid** | On empty stomach if sensitive; with iron-rich meals (inhibits iron absorption) |

## 6. Fermented Foods

**Dose:** 1–2 servings daily

### Ancient Source

| Source | Text | Reference |
|--------|------|-----------|
| Hippocrates, *On Regimen*, ~400 BC | "Soured milk and fermented drinks are more easily digested and strengthen the body." | Book II |
| Avicenna, *Canon of Medicine*, 1025 AD | "Fermented milk products improve digestion, strengthen the stomach, and are beneficial for the constitution." | Book III |
| *Charaka Samhita*, ~400 BC (Ayurveda) | "Dadhi (fermented milk) is wholesome, strengthening, and promotes digestive fire when taken appropriately." | Sutrasthana Chapter 27 |

### Modern Evidence

| Study | Finding | Reference |
|-------|---------|-----------|
| Wastyk et al., 2021, *Cell* (Stanford) | 10-week fermented food intervention increased microbiome diversity, decreased 19 inflammatory proteins (including IL-6) | PMID: 34256014 |
| Asnicar et al., 2021, *Nature* | Microbiome diversity is one of the strongest biomarkers of health and longevity; centenarians have distinctive microbiome signatures | PMID: 33432175 |
| Hill et al., 2014, *Nat Rev Gastroenterol Hepatol* | Probiotic/fermented food consumption supports gut barrier function, immune modulation, mental health via gut-brain axis | PMID: 24912386 |

**Evidence Summary:** ★★★★☆ (Very Good) — Strong mechanistic and emerging interventional evidence.

### Mechanism

- **Live cultures:** Directly seed beneficial bacteria (Lactobacillus, Bifidobacterium)
- **Postbiotics:** Metabolites (SCFAs, bacteriocins) modulate immune function
- **Microbiome diversity:** Competitive exclusion of pathogens; increased SCFA production (butyrate)
- **Gut-brain axis:** 70% of immune cells in gut; vagal signaling to brain

### Protocol

| Food | Serving | Notes |
|------|---------|-------|
| Kefir | 1 cup | Highest probiotic diversity (30+ strains) |
| Sauerkraut (unpasteurized) | ½ cup | Rich in Lactobacillus, vitamin C |
| Kimchi | ½ cup | Additional capsacin, vegetable nutrients |
| Yogurt (live culture) | 1 cup | Most accessible, widely available |
| Miso | 1 tbsp in broth | Additional isoflavones |
| Natto | 1 pack (40 g) | Highest vitamin K2 (menaquinone-7) source |
| Kombucha | 1 cup | Lower probiotic count, but diverse organic acids |

## 7. Whole Grains

**Dose:** 3+ servings daily (target: 30+ g fiber/day total)

### Ancient Source

| Source | Text | Reference |
|--------|------|-----------|
| Dioscorides, *De Materia Medica*, ~60 AD | "Wheat is the most nutritious grain. Barley is cooling, loosens the bowels, and is less heating than wheat." | Book II, Chapter 79 |
| Hippocrates, *On the Regimen*, ~400 BC | "Barley water is the most suitable drink for all seasons and ages." | Book II |

### Modern Evidence

| Study | Finding | Reference |
|-------|---------|-----------|
| Aune et al., 2016, meta-analysis (45 studies), *BMJ* | Each 90 g/day increase in whole grains associated with 17% lower all-cause mortality, 22% lower CVD mortality | PMID: 27301975 |
| Reynolds et al., 2019, *Lancet* (meta-analysis) | Highest fiber/whole grain intake associated with 15-30% lower all-cause and CVD mortality | PMID: 30638909 |
| Slavin, 2013, *Nutrients* | Whole grain fiber feeds beneficial gut bacteria → SCFA production → anti-inflammatory, anti-cancer, metabolic benefits | PMID: 23609775 |

**Evidence Summary:** ★★★★★ (Excellent) — Consistent across every major epidemiological study.

### Mechanism

- **Fiber:** Feeds beneficial microbiome → SCFA (butyrate) production → anti-inflammatory, colon cancer protection
- **Beta-glucan (oats, barley):** Cholesterol lowering via bile acid binding
- **Resistant starch:** Prebiotic; improves insulin sensitivity
- **B vitamins:** Energy metabolism, methylation, nervous system function

### Best Choices

| Grain | Key Benefit |
|-------|------------|
| **Oats** | Beta-glucan (cholesterol lowering) |
| **Quinoa** | Complete protein, mineral-dense |
| **Brown rice** | Resistant starch, B vitamins |
| **Barley** | Beta-glucan, fiber density |
| **Buckwheat** | Rutin (vascular health), gluten-free |
| **Farro/spelt** | Protein, fiber, traditional Mediterranean |

## 8. Berries

**Dose:** ½–1 cup daily

### Ancient Source

| Source | Text | Reference |
|--------|------|-----------|
| Dioscorides, *De Materia Medica*, ~60 AD | "Blackberries are slightly astringent and cooling. They strengthen the stomach and are good for the mouth and throat." | Book IV, Chapter 76 |
| Avicenna, *Canon of Medicine*, 1025 AD | "Berries are cold and dry, they strengthen the stomach and are beneficial for hot conditions." | Book II |

### Modern Evidence

| Study | Finding | Reference |
|-------|---------|-----------|
| Cassidy et al., 2013, *Ann Neurol* | ≥2 servings/week of blueberries/strawberries associated with up to 2.5 years delay in cognitive aging | PMID: 22535616 |
| Basu et al., 2010, *Am J Clin Nutr* | Blueberry consumption for 8 weeks improved insulin sensitivity and reduced inflammatory markers | PMID: 27916833 |
| Wallace & Giusti, 2015, *J Agric Food Chem* | Anthocyanins from berries reduce oxidative DNA damage, improve vascular endothelial function | PMID: 26374184 |

**Evidence Summary:** ★★★★☆ (Very Good) — Strong observational evidence, growing interventional data.

### Mechanism

- **Anthocyanins:** Cross blood-brain barrier; reduce neuroinflammation; improve vascular function via eNOS activation
- **Ellagic acid:** Anti-cancer (induces apoptosis in malignant cells, inhibits angiogenesis)
- **Quercetin:** Anti-inflammatory, senolytic properties (clears senescent cells)
- **Pterostilbene (blueberries):** Resveratrol analog with superior bioavailability

### Quick Reference

| | |
|---|---|
| **Best** | Wild blueberries (2× antioxidants of cultivated), blackberries, black currants |
| **How** | Fresh or frozen (frozen retains nutrients); smoothies, oatmeal, alone |
| **Evidence** | ★★★★☆ |
| **Ancient source** | Dioscorides, Avicenna |
